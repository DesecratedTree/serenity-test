Cooking - Cooking Methods
Introduction | Baking | Brewing - Members Only | Churning - Members Only

Introduction

There are many different methods of Cooking and a variety of base ingredients to use to make your foods complete. As your skill increases, you will find yourself able to make more complicated types of food. For the skill requirements of these foods please see the Non-Members' or Members' food sections.

Baking

Making flour
Dough is the basic ingredient for many recipes within RuneScape, so it is important that you can make it. Dough is composed of both flour and water. To get water, fill a bucket or a jug from any source of water. Look for the following symbol on your minimap .

To make flour you first need to gather grain from a field, then, after making sure you have an empty pot in your inventory, find a windmill. Windmills are located on the map by the windmill symbol .

Once in a windmill, go to the top floor and use the grain with the hopper. Click on the controls to operate them, then go down to the bottom floor and collect the flour from the flour bin into your empty pot.

Use the pot of flour with the bucket (or jug) of water. You will then get the option to make bread dough, pitta bread dough, pastry dough, or pizza dough.

Bread Pitta Bread

  1. Make dough using the instructions above and select bread dough.
  2. Use the bread dough with a Cooking range.

  1. Make dough using the instructions above and select pitta bread dough.
  2. Use the pitta bread dough with a Cooking range.
Pies Pizza

  1. Make dough using the instructions above and select pastry dough.
  2. Use the pastry dough with an empty pie dish and add a filling of your choice. You can obtain cooking apples from the Cooking Guild and the Ardougne Farming shop. You can obtain redberries south of Varrock.
  3. Use the pie with a Cooking range.

  1. Make dough using the instructions above and select pizza dough.
  2. Get a tomato and use it with the pizza base. Then use cheese with the incomplete pizza.
  3. Use the uncooked pizza with a Cooking range.
  4. Add your choice of topping: anchovy, meat or pineapple. You can catch anchovies using a Fishing net in one of the Fishing spots around RuneScape. You can obtain a pineapple from the Grand Tree in the Tree Gnome Stronghold, or they can be picked from pineapple plants west of Brimhaven on Karamja.
Cake Chocolate Cake

  1. Mix a pot of flour, an egg and a bucket of milk into a cake tin to make an uncooked cake.
  2. Use the uncooked cake on a range to bake a cake.

  1. Follow the instructions to make a cake.
  2. Use a chocolate bar with the cake to make a chocolate cake.


Brewing - Members Only

Hops are an important ingredient in the brewing process. Hops can be grown through Farming or bought from some Farming shops. With hops, cooks can use their skills to brew a selection of drinks, either saving them the costs of buying them from a bar, or making an entirely new drink, which can be used, or sold to players to help temporarily boost skills.

Through brewing you are introduced to a variety of alcohols which can be brewed in the breweries of Keldagrim and Port Phasmatys.

How to Brew

To brew, you will need to obtain barley and a pot of ale yeast. The barley can be bought from the Farming shop north of Port Sarim, while the pot of ale yeast can be bought from the local brewers.


+
+ Main ingredient + Into
  1. Add two buckets of water to a fermenting vat.
  2. Add two lots of barley malt (this is made by using barley on a Cooking range) to the fermenting vat.
  3. Add the main ingredient to the fermenting vat.
  4. Lastly, add a pot of ale yeast.
  5. Wait for several days (depending on the type of drink you are brewing).
  6. Turn the valve to let the liquid run into the barrel.
  7. Now simply use either a calquat keg or a beer glass on the barrel to empty the contents.


How to Brew Cider

To start brewing you will need four buckets of apple mush. To obtain a bucket of apple mush, you will need four cooking apples, and an empty bucket to collect the apple mush in. All these items need to be in your inventory for you to successfully follow the apple mushing process detailed below.

+ =
  1. Use the cooking apples with the apple barrel (which can be found near a fermenting vat) and your character will begin to squash the apples.
  2. After the apples are squashed, the mush will automatically be collected into your empty bucket.
    Repeat steps 1 and 2 until you have enough buckets of apple mush.


Now you have collected your apple mush, you just need to grab a pot of ale yeast and you are ready to start brewing cider. Follow the recipe detailed below to begin.

+ Into
  1. First, add the buckets of apple mush to the fermenting vat.
  2. Add a pot of ale yeast
  3. Now sit back and wait.


When the yeast has been added, the mixture will begin to ferment. This process can take up to a day, so be prepared to just leave it and go do something else while you are waiting. Eventually, the ale or cider will be ready for drinking – turn the valve on the vat and the barrel will fill up. It is possible that occasionally your cider will turn bad while brewing - if this should prove to be the case, there is nothing you can do other than empty the vat and start again. You can then fill up to eight beer glasses from the barrel.

Alternatively, scoop the flesh out of a calquat fruit with a knife and you have a calquat keg that can hold four pints by itself!

Calquat fruits can be obtained by trading with another player or growing your own calquat tree and picking the fruit using the Farming skill.

Mature Ales

Mature ales are obtained when brewing. The chances of producing a mature ale when brewing is completely random, it does not depend on the amount of time the beer is left fermenting. You can only brew mature ales by brewing the normal states. For example, to obtain a mature Dragon Bitter, you will have to obtain the ingredients and brew Dragon Bitter. If you are lucky, you may obtain mature Dragon Bitter when it has fully fermented.

Due to the extra potency of a matured ale, you may find that the changes it makes to your skills will increase. For example, a mature Dwarven Stout will boost your Mining and Smithing levels by 2, instead of the standard 1.

The 'Stuff'

Those who have bought the 'stuff' from Honest Jimmy after participating in the Trouble Brewing will have an advantage when brewing ales (does not work on cider).

Follow the same steps as you do in normal brewing; however, after you have added the barley malt, add the 'stuff' to the vat, and then continue with the normal method again (add your hops, then yeast).

The 'stuff' will give you a 50% chance of brewing a mature ale of the type you are brewing.

Churning - Members Only

To make butter, you must first place milk into a large vat. Churn it around for a reasonable period of time in order to produce either cream, butter or cheese. These are all food which are added to other foods in order to make them taste better and restore more Hitpoints.

To use the churn, you must have a bucket of milk, a pot of cream, or a pat of butter within your inventory. Which items you have in your inventory will determine what you will be able to produce with the churn. There are two types of churn within RuneScape: wooden and metal. There is no difference between these two types of churns. Both can be found at the locations specified at the bottom of this page.

Item Level Required

Pot of cream		 21

Having a bucket of milk in your inventory will allow you to make a pot of cream, a pat of butter and some cheese. Having a pot of cream in your inventory will allow you to make a pat of butter or some cheese. Having a pat of butter in your inventory will allow you to make some cheese.

Locations of Churns

You can find the churns in the following locations:


  • Inside the Make Over Mage's house. Nearest dairy cows are next to the Crafting Guild.
  • Inside Frenita's restaurant in Yanille. Nearest dairy cow is north of Yanille.
  • Inside the mill in Zanaris. Nearest dairy cow is in a field in Zanaris.
  • In a farm building, north of Ardougne. Nearest dairy cow is in cow field nearby.
  • In building next to fenced area with cow and chickens in Rellekka. Nearest dairy cow is in this fenced area.
  • Next to the shed in Sinclair Mansion garden. Nearest dairy cow is in the south west of the garden.
  • In farm building south of Falador. Nearest dairy cow is in the field, north of the farmhouse.
  • In farm building to the north-west of Lumbridge. Nearest dairy cow is to the north of the nearby windmill.
  • In farm building to the north of Lumbridge, opposite a large field full of cows. Nearest dairy cow is in said field.
  • On the first floor of the Cooking Guild. Not particularly near any cows - the nearest dairy cows are probably the Lumbridge or Falador ones.



Baked Potatoes


As well as being given a new technique, players can also create a different selection of food, using the new items they create when churning.

Using a potato on a Cooking range will make a baked potato. Baked potatoes cannot be made using a fire, they will always burn - please see the Members Food section for details of the required level to cook the different variations of potatoes.


Buttered Potato Potato with Cheese
To make a buttered potato, use a pat of butter with a baked potato.

  1. To make a potato with cheese, follow the instructions to make a buttered potato.
  2. Use some cheese with the buttered potato.
Chilli Potato Egg Potato

  1. To make a chilli potato, follow the instructions to make a buttered potato.
  2. Use the buttered potato with a bowl of chilli con carne (see below for instructions to make chilli con carne).

  1. To make an egg potato, follow the instructions to make a buttered potato.
  2. Use the buttered potato with a bowl of scrambled egg and tomato (for instructions to make scrambled egg and tomato see below).
Mushroom Potato Tuna Potato

  1. To make a mushroom potato, follow the instructions to make a buttered potato.
  2. Use the buttered potato with a bowl of fried mushrooms and fried onions (see below for instructions to make fried mushrooms and fried onions).

  1. To make a tuna potato, follow the instructions to make a buttered potato.
  2. Use the buttered potato with a bowl of tuna and sweetcorn (for instructions to make tuna and sweetcorn see below).


Miscellaneous Methods

Fish Meat

  1. To cook fish, obtain the fish that you want to cook by fishing for it, or by buying some raw fish from the fishing shop in Port Sarim.
  2. Use your chosen fish with a Cooking range or an open fire.

  1. To cook any meat, obtain the meat that you want to cook by killing an animal for it or picking it up from a monster drop.
  2. Use your chosen meat with a Cooking range or an open fire.
Ugthanki Kebab Oomlie Wrap

  1. Obtain an empty bowl and have a knife in your inventory.
  2. Use a tomato on the bowl to get a bowl of chopped tomatoes.
  3. Use an onion on the bowl of chopped tomatoes to get a bowl of chopped tomatoes and onions.
  4. Obtain a piece of raw ugthanki meat. This can be found from monster drops (usually in the desert south of Shantay Pass).
  5. Cook the meat on a fire or a Cooking range and add it to the bowl of chopped tomatoes and onions.
  6. This will give you a kebab mix.
  7. Make pitta bread using the instructions above.
  8. Use the kebab mix with the pitta bread to make an ugthanki kebab.



  1. Obtain some oomlie meat by killing oomlie birds.
  2. Obtain a palm leaf by shaking a leafy palm tree.
  3. Use the oomlie meat and palm leaf together to make an uncooked oomlie wrap.
  4. Use the uncooked oomlie wrap with a range.

Stew Curry

  1. Obtain an empty bowl and fill it with water from a water source.
  2. Pick a potato from a field and use it with the bowl of water.
  3. Cook some meat and use it in the incomplete stew. This will make an uncooked stew.
  4. Use the uncooked stew with a Cooking range.

  1. Follow the instructions to make an uncooked stew and add 3 curry leaves or a pot of spices.
  2. Use the uncooked curry with a Cooking range.
Chilli Con Carne Scrambled Egg and Tomato

  1. The first ingredient is minced meat, which is made by adding cooked meat to a bowl (with a knife in your inventory).
  2. The second ingredient is spicy sauce, which is made by adding garlic to a bowl (with a knife in your inventory) to get chopped garlic.
  3. Add some gnome spice to the chopped garlic.
  4. Use the minced meat with the spicy sauce.

  1. The first ingredient is scrambled egg, which is made by using an egg on a bowl (to create a bowl of uncooked egg) and then using it on a range to make scrambled eggs.
  2. The second ingredient is chopped tomato, which is made by adding a tomato to the bowl (with a knife in your inventory).
  3. Then use the bowl of chopped tomatoes with the bowl of scrambled egg.
Fried Mushrooms and Fried Onions Tuna and Sweetcorn

  1. The first ingredient is fried mushrooms, which is made by using a bittercap mushroom with a bowl (with a knife in your inventory) to get sliced mushrooms.
  2. Then use the bowl of sliced mushrooms with a Cooking range to make fried mushrooms.
  3. The second ingredient is fried onions, which is made by using an onion with a bowl (with a knife in your inventory).
  4. Use the bowl of sliced onions on a range to make fried onions.
  5. Then use the fried onions with the fried mushrooms.

  1. The first ingredient is chopped tuna, which is made by adding cooked tuna to a bowl (with a knife in your inventory).
  2. Then use a piece of cooked sweetcorn with the bowl of cooked tuna (this can be done without a knife in your inventory).
